Keep Your Vehicle Running Smoothly

Essential Car Maintenance Tips

Regular maintenance is crucial for keeping your vehicle safe, reliable, and running at peak performance. Follow these essential maintenance tasks to extend your car's lifespan.

Oil Changes

Frequency: Every 3,000-7,000 miles (depending on oil type and vehicle)
Oil changes are one of the most important maintenance tasks. Fresh oil keeps your engine lubricated and prevents premature wear. Always use the grade recommended in your owner's manual.

Tire Maintenance

Frequency: Monthly pressure checks, rotation every 6,000-8,000 miles
Check tire pressure monthly and rotate tires regularly to ensure even wear. Proper tire maintenance improves fuel efficiency and safety. Replace tires when tread depth reaches 2/32 inch.

Air Filter Replacement

Frequency: Every 15,000-30,000 miles
A clean air filter improves engine performance and fuel efficiency. Inspect your air filter every oil change and replace it when it becomes clogged with dirt and debris.

Brake Inspection

Frequency: Every 50,000 miles or as needed
Regular brake inspections ensure your safety. Check brake pads for wear, inspect rotors for damage, and have brake fluid checked and replaced according to manufacturer specifications.

Battery Check

Frequency: Annually, especially before winter
Check your battery terminals for corrosion and ensure they're clean and tight. Most batteries last 3-5 years. Have your battery tested regularly to avoid unexpected failures.

Fluid Levels

Frequency: Monthly
Check coolant, transmission fluid, brake fluid, and windshield washer fluid regularly. Low fluid levels can indicate leaks or other problems that need attention.
